## Rosedale

Rosedale is a neighborhood in New York City in the southeastern portion of the borough of Queens . The neighborhood, located along the southern part of Queens , borders Nassau County .

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Rosedale is a southeastern Queens neighborhood at the Nassau County border with auto-oriented access via several major arterials and parkways, and commuter rail service via the Long Island Rail Road.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Despite its edge-of-city location, Rosedale benefits from strong connectivity to both Queens and Nassau County through Sunrise Highway, Rockaway Boulevard, Merrick Boulevard, and nearby JFK Airport.


- **THE MARKET:** The area has a suburban character shaped by mid-20th-century development, with commercial uses concentrated along its major boulevards and corridors that link Queens to adjacent Nassau County communities.



---

### Around the Block

Rosedale occupies a strategic position at the eastern edge of New York City, where Queens meets Nassau County, and this border location strongly influences its commercial profile. Historically, the neighborhood’s transformation from isolated farmland into a more developed community followed the arrival of the Southern Railroad of Long Island, now the Long Island Rail Road. The Laurelton station once served the broader area that includes today’s Rosedale, Laurelton, and Springfield Gardens, helping to set the stage for subsequent growth and roadway construction. As roads like Francis Lewis Boulevard and Sunrise Boulevard (now Sunrise Highway) were built out, they created critical east–west and north–south corridors that continue to support movement of people and goods between Queens and Long Island. Over time, the farmland persisted into the mid‑1930s, after which development rapidly shifted the area into a suburban-style neighborhood with commercial activity clustering along its more heavily trafficked routes.

Today, Rosedale’s commercial connectivity is reinforced by several key transportation assets. The neighborhood is served by the Rosedale station on the Long Island Rail Road’s Atlantic Branch, with service from both the Far Rockaway and Long Beach branches, which supports commuter access to employment centers in Brooklyn and Manhattan as well as Nassau County. Extensive bus coverage—via the Q5, Q85, Q86, Q87, Q89, Q111, and the rush‑hour QM63 express bus—enhances local circulation and links Rosedale to other parts of southeast Queens. Major roadways including Francis Lewis Boulevard, Conduit Avenue, Rockaway Boulevard, Merrick Boulevard, Sunrise Highway, the Cross Island Parkway, and the Belt Parkway knit the neighborhood into the broader regional road network and facilitate retail and service-oriented uses along these thoroughfares. Subdistricts such as Warnerville and Meadowmere, situated near Jamaica Bay and Hook Creek and adjacent to JFK Airport and Nassau County, underscore Rosedale’s interface with waterfront, aviation, and suburban markets. Brookville Park on the western border, while primarily recreational, also adds to the neighborhood’s appeal as a setting for community-serving retail and institutional uses nearby. Overall, Rosedale combines border‑location access, rail and bus infrastructure, and multiple major roads to support a modest but regionally connected commercial environment.
