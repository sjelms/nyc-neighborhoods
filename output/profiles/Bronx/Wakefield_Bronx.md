## Wakefield

Wakefield is a working-class and middle-class section of the northern borough of the Bronx in New York City . It is bounded by the city's border with Westchester County to the north, East 222nd Street to the south, and the Bronx River Parkway to the west.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Wakefield is a northern Bronx neighborhood with a mix of local retail, institutional uses such as schools and a library, and some legacy industrial and factory buildings along corridors like Bronx Boulevard.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Transit options are unusually robust for a border neighborhood, with multiple subway stations, extensive city and Westchester bus service, and a Metro-North Harlem Line stop supporting worker and customer access.


- **THE MARKET:** As a working- and middle-class area with steady population density and moderate median income, Wakefield supports neighborhood-serving retail and service businesses along streets such as White Plains Road and East 233rd Street, with potential for adaptive reuse of older commercial and factory properties.



---

### Around the Block

Wakefield occupies the northern edge of the Bronx, directly along the border with Westchester County, which makes it a gateway between New York City and adjacent suburban markets. Historically, the area shifted from forest to farmland and then to a more built-up district after the arrival of the New York and Harlem Railroad around 1840, setting a long-term pattern of rail-oriented development. A factory building on Bronx Boulevard hints at the neighborhood’s light industrial and manufacturing legacy, while current land use includes community anchors like the New York Public Library’s Wakefield branch and a concentration of educational institutions. These include several public schools and prominent private and Catholic schools such as Mount Saint Michael Academy, which draws students from a wider catchment and brings daily foot traffic and employment to the area.

Commercially, Wakefield is characterized by neighborhood-serving retail strips and service businesses interspersed with residential blocks. White Plains Road functions as a key spine, hosting the Wakefield Station post office and long-standing establishments such as Cullen’s Tavern, a bar that has attracted film and television productions. Transit access strongly supports commerce and labor mobility: the 2 and 5 subway lines stop at multiple stations from 225th Street up to Wakefield–241st Street, giving the area direct links along the Bronx’s central spine and into Manhattan. The Metro-North Harlem Line’s Wakefield station adds a commuter-rail dimension, connecting the neighborhood to employment centers in Westchester and Midtown. An extensive bus network overlays this rail access, including several Bronx local and express routes plus Bee-Line buses to Yonkers, Bronxville, White Plains, Valhalla, and New Rochelle, tying Wakefield into regional shopping, employment, and institutional hubs. Public safety and basic services are supported by local NYPD and FDNY facilities, which help sustain a stable environment for small businesses and institutional operators. Overall, Wakefield’s combination of density, strong transit connectivity, and legacy commercial and industrial buildings underpins a neighborhood economy oriented toward everyday retail, education, and service uses, with cross-border access to Westchester markets.
