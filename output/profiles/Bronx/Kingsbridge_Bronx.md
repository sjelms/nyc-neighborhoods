## Kingsbridge

Kingsbridge is a residential neighborhood in the northwest portion of the Bronx , New York City . Kingsbridge's boundaries are Manhattan College Parkway to the north, the Major Deegan Expressway or Bailey Avenue to the east, West 230th Street to the south, and Irwin Avenue to the west.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Kingsbridge is a northwest Bronx neighborhood with a significant retail core supported by a business improvement district encompassing about 200 merchants, making it one of the largest shopping districts in the borough.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Despite its dense built form and steep "step streets" connecting to Riverdale, Kingsbridge retains active recreational assets and institutional anchors, including Gaelic Park, local Roman Catholic churches and schools, and Manhattan College.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial activity centers on a large BID-backed shopping district with proximity to the River Plaza Shopping Center in nearby Marble Hill, supported by strong bus and subway access and stable, moderate household incomes in the broader community district.



---

### Around the Block

Kingsbridge functions as an important commercial and institutional hub in the northwest Bronx, layered over a long transportation-oriented history. The neighborhood’s origins are tied to the King’s Bridge, once part of the Boston Post Road and a key link between what is now the Bronx and Marble Hill; that early infrastructure set a precedent for Kingsbridge’s role as a connector. While the original creek and bridge have been buried and redirected into a modern shipping canal, their legacy informs a geography still centered on major corridors such as Broadway and 230th Street. Today, these corridors and the surrounding streets support one of the Bronx’s largest retail shopping districts, organized through a business improvement district that encompasses roughly 200 merchants. This concentration of storefronts, services, and restaurants draws customers from neighboring Riverdale, Marble Hill, and other west Bronx communities, anchoring Kingsbridge as a day-to-day shopping destination.

Transit and institutional uses reinforce this commercial base. Two nearby subway stations on the 1 line at 231st Street and 238th Street connect Kingsbridge directly to Manhattan and other Bronx neighborhoods, while a dense network of local and express bus routes (Bx1, Bx3, Bx7, Bx9, Bx10, Bx20, and express routes BxM1, BxM2, BxM18) feed the retail corridors and support worker and customer access. Manhattan College, located in the neighborhood, functions as a major institutional anchor, bringing a steady population of students, staff, and visitors who rely on local businesses and services. Recreational facilities such as Gaelic Park on Broadway, operated by Manhattan College, and a lighted baseball field with concessions at 233rd Street and Bailey Avenue, add programmed foot traffic that can benefit nearby retail and food operators. Although River Plaza Shopping Center in adjacent Marble Hill falls outside the Kingsbridge BID, its proximity further concentrates regional shopping activity around the area. The neighborhood’s ongoing infrastructure investments, including improved parks and new elementary schools on 230th Street, suggest continued public-sector commitment, which can stabilize the environment for long-term commercial and institutional investment.

- **Boundaries:**  
  - **East to West:** Irwin Avenue on the west to the Major Deegan Expressway or Bailey Avenue on the east
  - **North to South:** Manhattan College Parkway on the north to West 230th Street on the south
  - **Adjacent Neighborhoods:** Kingsbridge Heights, Marble Hill, Riverdale, Riverdale-on-Hudson (historic Riverdale reference), Spuyten Duyvil
