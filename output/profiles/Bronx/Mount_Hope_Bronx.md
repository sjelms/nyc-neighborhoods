## Mount Hope

Tremont is a residential neighborhood in the West Bronx , New York City . Its boundaries are East 181st Street to the north, Third Avenue to the east, the Cross-Bronx Expressway to the south, and the Grand Concourse to the west. East Tremont Avenue is the primary thoroughfare through Tremont.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Mount Hope is referenced as part of Tremont’s historical identity, with Tremont functioning today as a dense West Bronx neighborhood organized around major arterials such as East Tremont Avenue and the Grand Concourse, and a street grid dominated by mid-rise buildings and institutional uses like schools, libraries, and a post office.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The area’s elevated, hilly terrain and its stair streets, together with historic brownstone blocks such as the Morris Avenue Historic District and early 20th-century civic buildings like the Carnegie library branches, add legacy character around otherwise utilitarian commercial corridors.


- **THE MARKET:** The neighborhood lies in a high-density market of more than 24,000 residents in roughly 0.3 square miles, with relatively low median household income, high rent burden, and visible signs of gentrification in Community District 6, supporting value-oriented local retail and service uses along East Tremont Avenue, Grand Concourse, and other bus- and subway-served corridors.

### Around the Block

Mount Hope is cited in the etymology of Tremont, where the neighborhood name was created from three areas: Mount Eden, Mount Hope, and Fairmount. In contemporary terms, the commercial and institutional life of the broader Tremont area frames the context for Mount Hope. East Tremont Avenue operates as the primary thoroughfare, forming a spine of neighborhood retail, services, and local traffic. The Grand Concourse, a major Bronx arterial, runs along the western edge of Tremont and supports both vehicular and subway access via the IND Concourse Line. These corridors give businesses in and around Mount Hope visibility to heavy pedestrian and bus flows, as well as north–south mobility across the West Bronx. The area’s land use is heavily shaped by 5- and 6-story apartment houses, which concentrate population and create a consistent local customer base for ground-floor commercial and community facilities.

The neighborhood’s topography is notably elevated and very hilly, with stair streets linking different elevations. This physical form constrains large-format development but encourages small- to mid-scale, walkable retail nodes at key intersections and transit stops. Historic resources such as the Morris Avenue Historic District, lined with attached brownstones, and the early-1900s Tremont branch of the New York Public Library, contribute to a legacy streetscape that can be attractive for small offices, professional services, and neighborhood-oriented businesses operating out of older buildings. Public facilities—including multiple public schools, parochial schools, fire stations, and a post office on East Tremont Avenue—anchor daily traffic and daytime population. Health and social indicators show concentrated need, which underpins demand for clinics, social services, and affordable food outlets; supermarkets are relatively scarce compared to bodegas. Transit connectivity is robust, with several bus routes and subway stations, plus a Metro-North Harlem Line stop at Tremont, enhancing accessibility for workers, customers, and service providers. Altogether, the environment around Mount Hope is that of a dense, transit-served West Bronx district where small-scale retail, community facilities, and legacy housing dominate the commercial fabric.
