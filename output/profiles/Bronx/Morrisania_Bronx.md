## Morrisania

Morrisania ( MORR -i- SAY -nee-ə ) is a residential neighborhood in the southwestern Bronx, New York City, New York. Its boundaries are the Cross Bronx Expressway to the north, Crotona-Prospect Avenue to the east, East 163rd Street to the south, and Webster Avenue to the west. Third Avenue is the primary thoroughfare through Morrisania.

### Around the Block

Morrisania sits in the southwestern Bronx with a long evolution from estate lands to rail-linked town to contemporary South Bronx neighborhood. Its current landscape is dominated by NYCHA public housing complexes of varying heights and scales, interspersed with rehabilitated tenement buildings and newer subsidized townhouses. Third Avenue functions as the primary thoroughfare, supporting local commercial activity and linking the neighborhood to other Bronx business nodes such as The Hub and Fordham Plaza via the Bx15 bus. The terrain is somewhat hilly, and much of the original multi-unit housing stock that once included smaller homes and early tenements was heavily damaged by arson in the 1970s and later demolished, clearing many of the vacant lots that city-sponsored projects now occupy.

Historically, rail infrastructure and later elevated and subway extensions helped transform Morrisania from a bedroom community for Manhattan workers into a full-fledged town with its own local industries and craftsmen. Although the Third Avenue Elevated closed in 1973, the district still relies on transit connectivity for its workforce and customer base. Multiple bus routes (Bx6/SBS, Bx11, Bx15, Bx21, Bx35, Bx41/SBS) provide crosstown access and connections to major subway hubs on the 2, 5, and 6 lines, making it easier for residents and employees to commute across the borough and into Manhattan. The Cross Bronx Expressway forms the neighborhood’s northern boundary, acting as a major regional freight and vehicular route that frames the district even though there is no subway station inside Morrisania itself. Within the neighborhood, institutional uses such as Morris High School and the Forest Houses-adjacent Morris High School Historic District, as well as several NYPD, FDNY, EMS, library, and postal facilities, anchor daily foot traffic and service employment. Historic blocks along Clay Avenue and around Morris High School add an older urban fabric that contrasts with the surrounding towers-in-the-park style complexes, creating pockets of intact early Bronx architecture that could support niche commercial and community uses at the ground floor where zoning and building configuration allow.
