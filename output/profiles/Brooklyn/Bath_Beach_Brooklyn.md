## Bath Beach

Bath Beach is a neighborhood in the New York City borough of Brooklyn , located at the southwestern edge of the borough on Gravesend Bay . The neighborhood borders Bensonhurst and New Utrecht to the northeast across 86th Street; Dyker Beach Park and Golf Course to the northwest across 14th Avenue; and Gravesend to the east across Stillwell Avenue .

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** A working-class Brooklyn waterfront neighborhood with semi-attached and small apartment buildings, multiple commercial corridors and a mix of independent retailers and chains concentrated at Ceasar's Bay Shopping Center, 86th Street, and Bath Avenue.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** A legacy as a historic seaside resort and amusement area now overlaid with mid-20th-century infrastructure, including the Shore/Belt Parkway, promenade, and ballfields, supporting both local recreation and regional vehicular access.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial activity is driven by neighborhood-serving mom-and-pop businesses and national retailers in shopping center formats, supported by strong transit connectivity via multiple subway stations and bus routes along key avenues.

### Around the Block

Bath Beach sits on the southwestern edge of Brooklyn along Gravesend Bay, where its commercial character grew out of a 19th‑century seaside resort for affluent families. The area historically hosted its own amusement park and beach, which has since been paved over to create the Shore Parkway and associated waterfront promenade. This infrastructure reshaped the shoreline into a corridor of recreation fields, open space, and auto-oriented retail, including the Ceasar's Bay shopping center. The shopping center, located at the terminus of Bay Parkway and extended from Bensonhurst Park with land excavated for the Shore (Belt) Parkway, is now one of the neighborhood’s primary retail anchors. It concentrates chain stores that draw from a broad trade area beyond Bath Beach itself.

Away from the shoreline, the commercial grid is supported by a network of notable two-way thoroughfares. Cropsey Avenue, Bath Avenue, Benson Avenue, and 86th Street run parallel to the parkway and provide continuous frontage for ground-floor retail and services. 86th Street in particular has long served as a recognizable commercial strip, prominent enough to be used as a filming location for multiple major films and television series, underscoring its visibility and pedestrian traffic. Bath Avenue also supports a variety of local businesses, including mom-and-pop operations that cater to the neighborhood’s diverse customer base. The cross-streets, labeled as "Bay" numbered streets and intersecting with two-way avenues such as 14th through 28th Avenues and Bay Parkway, create an organized grid that distributes local traffic to these corridors. This structure, combined with waterfront road access via the Belt Parkway, enables efficient movement of shoppers, employees, and goods through the district.

Transit further reinforces Bath Beach’s commercial viability. The neighborhood is served by the D, R, and W subway services on the BMT West End Line, with several stations—18th Avenue, 20th Avenue, Bay Parkway, 25th Avenue, and Bay 50th Street—situated to feed foot traffic into adjacent retail stretches. Multiple bus routes, including local lines and express services like the X28 and X38, connect Bath Beach to other Brooklyn neighborhoods and Manhattan, broadening the labor and customer catchment for local businesses. Parks such as Bensonhurst Park, the Shore Parkway promenade and bike path, and several playgrounds add recreational draws that complement nearby commercial uses, from food and beverage to convenience retail. All of this activity is embedded in a working-class residential context with a significant immigrant population, which sustains a dense mix of small-scale enterprises interspersed with larger regional retailers.

### Neighborhood Facts
- **Population:** 33,070
- **Population Density:** 62.3 inhabitants per acre (39,900/sq mi; 15,400/km2)
- **Area:** 480.37 acres (194.40 ha)
- **Boundaries:**  
  - **East to West:** Dyker Beach Park and Golf Course across 14th Avenue on the northwest to Gravesend Bay and the extended Bensonhurst Park/Ceasar's Bay shoreline on the southwest; the neighborhood borders Bensonhurst and New Utrecht to the northeast across 86th Street.
  - **North to South:** From 86th Street on the northeast southward toward Gravesend Bay and the Belt/Shore Parkway waterfront corridor; Stillwell Avenue forms the eastern edge across from Gravesend.
  - **Adjacent Neighborhoods:** Bensonhurst, Gravesend, New Utrecht
