## Prospect Heights

Prospect Heights is a neighborhood in the northwest of the New York City borough of Brooklyn . The traditional boundaries are Flatbush Avenue to the west, Atlantic Avenue to the north, Eastern Parkway – beginning at Grand Army Plaza – to the south, and Washington Avenue to the east. In the northern section of Prospect Heights are the Vanderbilt Rail Yards, built over as part of the Pacific Park (formerly Atlantic Yards) project. The Barclays Center , home to the NBA 's Brooklyn Nets basketball team, is located in the northwestern corner of the neighborhood in Pacific Park at Flatbush and Atlantic Avenues.

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Prospect Heights is a compact northwest Brooklyn neighborhood with major institutional anchors along Eastern Parkway and an intensively redeveloped northern edge tied to the Pacific Park (Atlantic Yards) project and Barclays Center.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Former bakeries and factory spaces along Pacific Street have been converted into lofts, and a thriving commercial zone with bars, restaurants, and specialty shops has emerged along Vanderbilt and Washington Avenues.


- **THE MARKET:** The commercial environment includes arena-driven traffic at Barclays Center, historic-building constraints in much of the interior, and ongoing high-rise, luxury residential development with associated ground-floor retail and commercial space as part of the Pacific Park project.

### Around the Block

Prospect Heights presents a layered commercial and institutional landscape shaped by its position at the junction of Flatbush Avenue, Atlantic Avenue, and Eastern Parkway. Along its southern boundary, Eastern Parkway functions as a major cultural corridor, with the Brooklyn Public Library, Brooklyn Botanic Garden, and Brooklyn Museum drawing year-round visitor traffic and supporting surrounding retail and service uses. The northern edge of the neighborhood includes the Vanderbilt Rail Yards, now being built over as part of the Pacific Park (formerly Atlantic Yards) development, which is planned to deliver a mix of housing and commercial space in a cluster of high-rise buildings. The Barclays Center arena, home of the Brooklyn Nets, anchors the northwest corner at Flatbush and Atlantic Avenues, concentrating event-based foot traffic and transit riders in and around that intersection. These regional draws position Prospect Heights as a node where entertainment, cultural, and institutional uses intersect with neighborhood-scale commerce.

Within the neighborhood, land use shifts quickly from large-scale civic and arena facilities to historic, lower-scale building stock. Much of the interior consists of brownstone-style row houses and some larger multi-unit apartment buildings, many dating to the 1890s, within a large historic district that limits certain types of redevelopment but can favor well-executed adaptive reuse. Along Pacific Street between Vanderbilt and Carlton Avenues, defunct bakeries and factory buildings have been renovated into lofts, signaling a transition away from legacy industrial use toward residential and creative or live-work occupancy; some parcels have been acquired or cleared in anticipation of further Pacific Park build-out. Vanderbilt Avenue and Washington Avenue have become the primary neighborhood commercial corridors, with a concentration of restaurants, bars, and specialty retailers, including higher-end concepts such as a Michelin-affiliated restaurant and gourmet-focused shops. At Grand Army Plaza, an upscale glass high-rise designed by Richard Meier adds contemporary residential density at a key gateway location, benefiting from visibility and access. Transit and bus service along Flatbush Avenue, Atlantic Avenue, and key local streets supports these uses, feeding both daily neighborhood demand and large spikes in visitors tied to arena events and cultural institutions.
