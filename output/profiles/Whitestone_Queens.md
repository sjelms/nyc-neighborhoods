## Whitestone

Whitestone is a residential neighborhood in the northernmost part of the New York City borough of Queens . The neighborhood proper is located between the East River to the north; College Point and Whitestone Expressway to the west; Flushing and 25th Avenue to the south; and Bayside and Francis Lewis Boulevard to the east.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Whitestone is a primarily residential neighborhood in northern Queens with limited commercial and industrial activity, historically influenced by the former Whitestone Branch of the Long Island Rail Road and later by highway infrastructure such as the Whitestone Expressway and the Bronx–Whitestone Bridge.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Despite its quiet residential character, Whitestone and its Malba subsection have attracted affluent investment and notable cultural and corporate occupants, including media, beverage, technology, and publishing firms that have operated from the area.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial uses are dispersed and secondary to housing, with legacy and current operators such as Fairchild Recording Equipment Corporation, Lee Kum Kee International Holdings Ltd., Glacéau, White Rock Beverages, World Journal, and Queens Tribune reflecting a mix of light industrial, office, and media uses rather than large-scale warehousing or manufacturing.



---

### Around the Block

Whitestone sits at the northern edge of Queens, directly facing the East River and linked to the Bronx via the Bronx–Whitestone Bridge. The neighborhood’s modern commercial profile has been shaped heavily by transportation infrastructure: the Belt Parkway and Whitestone Expressway trace part of the former Whitestone Branch rail right-of-way, and Interstate 678 now runs through the area to the bridge. This connection feeds directly into the Bruckner Interchange in the Bronx, tying Whitestone into a broader network of expressways, including the Cross Bronx Expressway, Bruckner Expressway, and Hutchinson River Parkway. That highway access, combined with proximity to the Flushing market, underpins Whitestone’s ability to support a variety of businesses despite its predominantly residential zoning. Historically, agriculture and large estates dominated, but by the late 19th and early 20th centuries, improved transit access attracted wealthy New Yorkers and incremental development.

While Whitestone does not host a dense central business district, it has been home to a diverse set of companies. The neighborhood has supported legacy and contemporary enterprises such as the Kinemacolor Company of America, Fairchild Recording Equipment Corporation founded by Sherman Fairchild, Lee Kum Kee International Holdings Ltd., Glacéau, and White Rock Beverages. Media and publishing presences have included World Journal and the Queens Tribune, indicating a capacity for office, back-office, and light production functions. The Malba subsection, considered one of the more affluent communities in Queens, consists mostly of large expensive homes rather than commercial stock, but the broader Whitestone area benefits from its image as an "elite enclave." Religious and community institutions like St. Luke's Roman Catholic Church, Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church, the Grace Episcopal Church on Clintonville Street, Whitestone Hebrew Centre, the Russian Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas, and the Holy Cross Greek Orthodox Church generate steady local foot traffic that supports nearby small-scale retail and services. Bus routes link Whitestone to Flushing–Main Street and the IRT Flushing Line, allowing workers and customers to reach the neighborhood without direct subway service, and the extensive express bus network connects it to Manhattan, broadening its commercial catchment.
