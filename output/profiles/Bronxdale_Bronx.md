## Bronxdale

Allerton is a working-class neighborhood geographically located in the East Bronx section of the Bronx , New York City . It is named in honor of Daniel Allerton, an early Bronx settler who purchased and farmed this area with his wife Hustace. It consists of two subsections called Bronxwood and Laconia . Its boundaries, starting from the north and moving clockwise, are Adee Avenue, Boston Road , and Gun Hill Road to the northeast; the IRT Dyre Avenue Line ( 5 train) to the east; Waring Avenue to the south; and Bronx River Parkway to the west. White Plains Road and Boston Road are the primary thoroughfares through Allerton.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Bronxdale is the northern, Allerton-area subsection historically identified by NYC Planning, within a working-class East Bronx district dominated by multi‑unit housing and several NYCHA developments that support a stable local customer base for neighborhood retail along White Plains Road and Boston Road.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Despite its dense, low and flat urban fabric with many tenements near the elevated line, the area benefits from multiple nearby hospitals, educational institutions, public libraries, and extensive bus and subway connectivity that draw steady daytime population and service demand.


- **THE MARKET:** Land use is predominantly multi‑unit residential with scattered tenements and large public housing complexes such as Eastchester Gardens and Parkside Houses, creating consistent, value‑oriented demand for convenience retail and service uses along major corridors like White Plains Road, Boston Road, Allerton Avenue, and Gun Hill Road.

### Around the Block

Bronxdale sits within the broader Allerton section of the East Bronx, an area framed by strong transit, working‑class demographics, and a predominantly multi‑unit housing stock. The terrain is low and flat and the total land area of the overall neighborhood is roughly two square miles, giving it a compact, urban feel that supports walkable retail and service activity. White Plains Road and Boston Road function as the primary thoroughfares, carrying both local and regional foot traffic and forming natural commercial spines for small businesses, storefront retail, and neighborhood services. Within the Allerton area are several NYCHA developments—including Eastchester Gardens and Parkside Houses in the northern and northwestern portions—that provide a large, concentrated residential base for nearby ground‑floor retail, bodegas, and everyday service tenants. Additional complexes such as Boston Road Plaza and Pelham Parkway Houses in adjacent subsections further reinforce a customer pool for discount retail, health‑care related offices, and convenience services along the major roads.

The neighborhood is tightly woven into a larger health‑care and institutional cluster that shapes local daytime activity. Nearby Morris Park houses major medical employers such as Calvary Hospital, Montefiore Medical Center’s Jack D. Weiler Hospital, NYC Health + Hospitals/Jacobi, and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine campus, all of which are easily accessed from Allerton and its northern subsections. This proximity brings employees, patients, and students through the area and helps sustain demand for food, pharmacy, and other service‑oriented commercial uses along key corridors and at bus and subway nodes. Public amenities, including the Allerton and Eastchester branches of the New York Public Library, add additional foot traffic and community‑oriented usage patterns that benefit nearby storefronts. The area’s heavy reliance on renting and the presence of large public housing developments support relatively stable occupancy and consumer demand, even as incomes remain modest. Overall, the Bronxdale/Allerton environment is characterized by transit‑served, medium‑ to high‑density housing, strong bus and subway access, and an embedded institutional ecosystem that collectively underpin neighborhood‑scale commercial and service opportunities rather than destination retail or large‑format industrial activity.

### Neighborhood Facts
- **Population:** Bronxwood (southern Allerton) has a population over 20,000; Laconia (northern Allerton) has a population under 25,000. The combined Allerton–Pelham Gardens neighborhood had 28,903 residents in 2010.
- **Population Density:** 39.8 inhabitants per acre (25,500/sq mi; 9,800/km²) for the combined Allerton–Pelham Gardens area.
- **Area:** 726.98 acres (294.20 ha) for the combined Allerton–Pelham Gardens area; total Allerton land area is roughly two square miles.
- **Boundaries:**  
  - **East to West:** For Allerton overall, bounded on the east by the IRT Dyre Avenue Line (5 train) and on the west by Bronx River Parkway.
  - **North to South:** For Allerton overall, bounded to the northeast by Adee Avenue, Boston Road, and Gun Hill Road, and to the south by Waring Avenue.
  - **Adjacent Neighborhoods:** Pelham Parkway, Williamsbridge
