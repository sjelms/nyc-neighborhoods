## Douglaston

Douglaston–Little Neck is a neighborhood in the northeastern part of the New York City borough of Queens . The community is located on the North Shore of Long Island , bordered to the east by the region of Great Neck in Nassau County , to the south by Glen Oaks and the North Shore Towers , and to the west by Bayside .

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Expect a low-density, auto-accessible Queens submarket with a suburban feel, anchored by Northern Boulevard retail nodes and the Douglaston Plaza Shopping Center, plus limited commercial strips along Douglaston Parkway and Little Neck Parkway.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The area combines active neighborhood retail with waterfront recreational assets, a golf course, yacht club, and multiple historic districts, appealing to destination dining, leisure, and institutional users rather than heavy industry.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial activity is driven by independently owned restaurants, pizzerias, delis, bodegas, and service retail clustered at key intersections and in the Douglaston Plaza Shopping Center, supported by strong household incomes and convenient access to the Long Island Expressway and parkways.



---

### Around the Block

Douglaston–Little Neck’s commercial character is shaped by a mix of neighborhood-serving retail and destination community facilities rather than large-scale office or industrial product. Northern Boulevard functions as the primary commercial corridor, historically developed into a commercial and cultural hub with venues like the former Little Neck Theater. Today, this spine, along with the intersections at Douglaston Parkway and Little Neck Parkway, concentrates many independently owned shops and restaurants, including traditional New York City–style pizzerias, delis, and bodegas. These smaller operators provide essential daily needs and support a steady local customer base, creating a stable, service-oriented retail environment.

A key component of the submarket is the Douglaston Plaza Shopping Center, described as a major hub of the community's economic activity. It houses a supermarket, additional retail outlets, and restaurants, acting as an anchor destination that complements the surrounding strip-retail fabric. The presence of a municipal 18‑hole golf course with a restaurant, the Douglaston Club, and the Douglaston Yacht Squadron adds a hospitality and recreation layer that can drive event, food-and-beverage, and seasonal demand. Cultural and civic institutions—such as the National Art League on Douglaston Parkway, the Immaculate Conception Center conference facility, active churches hosting performances, and local theater groups—bring periodic foot traffic and evening/weekend activity to the neighborhood. Transit access via two Long Island Rail Road stations on the Port Washington Branch, multiple local and express bus routes to Manhattan, and direct connections to Interstate 495, the Cross Island Parkway, and the Grand Central Parkway make the area reachable for both customers and employees, even in the absence of a subway station. Overall, the commercial landscape is low-rise and neighborhood-scaled, but well-supported by income levels, civic activity, and strong regional road connectivity.
