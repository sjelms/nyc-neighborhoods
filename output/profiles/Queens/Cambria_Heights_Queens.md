## Cambria Heights

Cambria Heights is a residential neighborhood in the southeastern portion of the New York City borough of Queens . It is bounded by Springfield Boulevard and Francis Lewis Boulevard to the west, the Elmont , Nassau County border on the east, Queens Village to the north, St. Albans to the west, and Montefiore Cemetery and Laurelton , Springfield Gardens , and Rosedale to the south. As of 2010, Cambria Heights's population was 18,677. The neighborhood is part of Queens Community Board 13 .

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Cambria Heights is a low-rise, predominantly residential neighborhood with limited but active commercial presence concentrated along key corridors such as Linden Boulevard, which hosts numerous storefront churches and local-serving businesses.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The neighborhood’s two recently designated historic districts showcase cohesive 1931 Storybook-style housing stock, enhancing streetscape character and preservation appeal even in an otherwise non-landmarked area.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial activity is primarily neighborhood-serving, with religious institutions, schools, and small storefront uses; no major industrial or warehouse clusters are noted, and land use is largely residential with institutional and faith-based facilities integrated into the fabric.



---

### Around the Block

Cambria Heights in southeastern Queens functions primarily as a residential enclave, but its commercial and institutional landscape plays an important role in neighborhood life. Linden Boulevard stands out as the key corridor, with many storefront churches from a variety of denominations and nondenominational groups anchoring local pedestrian traffic and small-scale commercial activity. This concentration of religious institutions, including the Sacred Heart Catholic Church and its affiliated Sacred Heart Catholic Academy, generates steady weekly visitation and supports a modest ecosystem of nearby services and retail. The presence of the Ohel, the resting place of the Lubavitcher Rebbe Rabbi Menachem M. Schneerson and his predecessor Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn, is a major draw, bringing tens of thousands of visitors from around the world for prayer and blessing and creating periodic spikes in local demand for transportation and neighborhood services.

The education sector is another defining institutional component of Cambria Heights. Public schools such as PS 176 Cambria Heights and PS/MS 147 Ronald McNair, along with multiple magnet high schools on the Andrew Jackson High School campus, form a sizable cluster of educational facilities that structure daily traffic patterns. Two private schools, Cambria Center for the Gifted Child and Cambria School of Excellence, add to this concentration, attracting families and employees and helping sustain daytime commercial demand. Historic preservation has begun to shape the built environment with the Cambria Heights–222nd Street and Cambria Heights–227th Street Historic Districts, both completed in 1931, which contain architecturally distinctive Storybook-style houses by firms such as Monda & Bertolazzi and Wolosoff Brothers. While these districts are residential, their landmark status adds stability and long-term predictability to the streetscape, indirectly influencing nearby small-business viability and investment decisions. Bus connectivity via several Queens routes and an express line links the neighborhood to the broader city, enabling residents, workers, students, and religious visitors to access Cambria Heights without reliance on heavy rail infrastructure, and supporting the steady, small-scale commercial ecosystem that characterizes the area.
