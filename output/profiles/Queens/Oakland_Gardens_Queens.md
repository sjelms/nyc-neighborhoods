## Oakland Gardens

Bayside is a neighborhood in the New York City borough of Queens . It is bounded by Whitestone to the northwest, the Long Island Sound and Little Neck Bay to the northeast, Douglaston to the east, Oakland Gardens to the south, and Fresh Meadows to the west. CNN Money ranked Bayside as one of the most expensive housing markets nationally when analyzing comparable detached homes. Despite its large housing stock of free-standing homes, it ranks high nationally in population density.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Oakland Gardens is a middle-class section of southern Bayside with a largely residential character and limited explicit commercial description, defined by major arterials and parkland edges that structure local traffic and small-scale neighborhood retail.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Its location between Alley Pond Park and Cunningham Park, along with proximity to Bayside’s broader retail, institutional, and recreational amenities, gives Oakland Gardens access to green space and services while remaining comparatively low-profile within the Bayside area.


- **THE MARKET:** Oakland Gardens shares ZIP Code 11364 with Bayside Hills and has a reported median income of $54,031, indicating a more moderate-income submarket within a generally higher-income Bayside trade area, with underlying demand supported by surrounding residential density.

### Around the Block

Oakland Gardens sits as the southern component of the larger Bayside area, and its land use is framed by major transportation and recreational corridors. To the north, the Long Island Expressway forms a clear boundary and connects the neighborhood to the rest of Queens and Long Island, serving as a primary conduit for residents, service providers, and any local businesses that depend on vehicular access. On its flanks, Alley Pond Park to the east and Cunningham Park to the west create green buffers that define the commercial catchment, constraining large-scale retail or industrial expansion but enhancing the appeal of neighborhood-serving uses. Within this framework, Bayside proper to the north acts as a larger commercial and institutional hub, with the Bayside Long Island Rail Road station connecting the area directly to New York Penn Station, Grand Central, northern Queens, and Long Island. Although not described with its own large retail corridors, Oakland Gardens effectively draws on these adjacent assets, especially Bell Boulevard, Bay Terrace, and other Bayside subareas that host shopping centers, restaurants, and service businesses.

The wider Bayside context also shapes the commercial profile of Oakland Gardens. Nearby Bay Terrace includes the Bay Terrace shopping center and several gated cooperative and condominium developments, which collectively generate stable consumer demand for neighborhood retail, dining, and personal services. Farther north, the presence of Fort Totten and the FDNY EMS Training Academy introduces public-sector employment and periodic visitor traffic into the broader Bayside market. Educational institutions such as Queensborough Community College, located on a former golf club site within Bayside, further drive weekday demand patterns for food service, convenience retail, and transportation-related businesses across the area. Extensive bus service, including multiple Q- and QM-series routes, as well as regional Nassau Inter-County Express routes, ties Oakland Gardens and southern Bayside into Flushing and other Queens employment centers, facilitating worker and customer flows. Major highways like the Cross Island Parkway, Clearview Expressway (I-295), and the Long Island Expressway (I-495) provide efficient regional access, which supports auto-oriented retail and service uses along key corridors, even as Oakland Gardens itself remains primarily middle-class and residential in character. As part of a high-income, low-crime district, the neighborhood benefits from the broader economic stability of Bayside and Douglaston–Little Neck, translating into a relatively secure environment for small-scale commercial activity at the edges of its parks and arterial roads.
