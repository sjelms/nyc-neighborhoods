## Little Neck

Douglaston–Little Neck is a neighborhood in the northeastern part of the New York City borough of Queens . The community is located on the North Shore of Long Island , bordered to the east by the region of Great Neck in Nassau County , to the south by Glen Oaks and the North Shore Towers , and to the west by Bayside .

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Douglaston–Little Neck offers a low-density, suburban-style commercial environment anchored by small neighborhood retail nodes at Douglaston Parkway and Little Neck Parkway along Northern Boulevard, plus the regional-serving Douglaston Plaza Shopping Center with a supermarket and multiple retail and restaurant tenants.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The area combines historic districts and waterfront open space with functioning commercial strips, a yacht club, golf course restaurant, and arts-oriented uses such as the National Art League, supporting a local cultural and leisure economy alongside everyday retail.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial activity is driven by independently owned restaurants, pizzerias, delis, bodegas, and service retail clustered around Northern Boulevard corridors, supplemented by shopping-center retail at Douglaston Plaza; zoning and land use are largely low-rise and auto-accessible, supported by proximity to major highways and commuter rail.



---

### Around the Block

Douglaston–Little Neck, which includes Little Neck on the east side of the district, is positioned on the North Shore of Long Island at the northeastern edge of Queens, where New York City transitions to Nassau County. The commercial pattern is built around Northern Boulevard, which historically evolved into a commercial and cultural hub with uses like the former Little Neck Theater at Northern Boulevard and Morgan Street and, today, multiple independently owned restaurants and shops near Douglaston Parkway and Little Neck Parkway. Retail here is neighborhood-focused, with traditional New York City–style pizzerias, delis, and bodegas providing everyday food and convenience services to the surrounding community. The National Art League occupies part of a block of shops on Douglaston Parkway, adding a cultural and gallery-like dimension to the retail fabric and supporting art classes and art sales. Douglaston Plaza Shopping Center further anchors the local economy, functioning as a major node that concentrates a supermarket, restaurants, and other retail outlets, drawing both neighborhood residents and customers from adjacent areas.

The district’s setting is framed by significant transportation and natural assets that influence its commercial and civic life. Two Long Island Rail Road Port Washington Branch stations, Douglaston and Little Neck, connect the neighborhood to Manhattan and western Long Island and sit near established commercial streets, enhancing commuter foot traffic for local businesses. Although there is no subway station, a network of bus routes—the Q12, Q30, Q36, plus QM5, QM8, QM35 express buses and n20G/n20X closed-door Nassau routes—links the area to the broader city and to Roslyn, helping sustain local retail and service businesses. On the vehicular side, Interstate 495 (the Long Island Expressway), the Cross Island Parkway, and the Grand Central Parkway provide direct car access that supports shopping-center and auto-oriented retail formats. The waterfront edge along Little Neck Bay hosts recreational and membership uses, including the Douglaston Yacht Squadron and the Douglaston Club, which drive demand for hospitality, events, and food-and-beverage operations. Nearby, the Douglaston Park Golf Course, a municipal 18-hole course with a restaurant and panoramic views, adds another hospitality and recreation venue that supports banquet, dining, and event-driven commercial activity. Together, these elements create a commercial ecosystem that is more suburban in form than much of New York City, but with longstanding transit links and civic institutions that sustain year-round economic activity.
