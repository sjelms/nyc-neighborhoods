## Baychester

Baychester is a neighborhood geographically located in the northeast part of the Bronx , New York City . Its boundaries are East 222nd Street to the northeast, the New England Thruway ( I-95 ) to the east, Gun Hill Road to the southwest, and Boston Road to the northwest. Eastchester Road is the primary thoroughfare through Baychester.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Baychester is a low-rise northeast Bronx neighborhood organized around Eastchester Road as its primary thoroughfare, with commercial services and institutional uses clustered along major corridors such as Boston Road, Gun Hill Road, and near Co-op City.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The area’s flat topography and legacy uses, including the former Freedomland U.S.A. amusement park site now occupied by Co-op City, create large, planned superblocks nearby that support schools, libraries, medical facilities, and retail destinations serving a broad trade area.


- **THE MARKET:** The neighborhood’s building stock is dominated by one- and two-family structures, with neighborhood retail, community facilities, and service uses embedded along key arterials; Baychester shares Community District 12’s relatively moderate median household incomes and is characterized as high-income relative to the rest of the city and not gentrifying.



---

### Around the Block

Baychester occupies a strategic position in the northeast Bronx, framed by key roadways and institutional anchors that shape its commercial environment. Eastchester Road functions as the primary thoroughfare, linking local retail, services, and community uses along a continuous corridor. Boston Road and Gun Hill Road also play important commercial roles, hosting post offices, bus routes, and neighborhood-serving storefronts that draw traffic from Baychester, neighboring Pelham Gardens, and Co-op City. The area’s overall terrain is low-lying and flat, an attribute that has historically supported large-format development, including the now-vanished Freedomland U.S.A. amusement park. That site has since been redeveloped as Co-op City, a massive residential complex whose scale and population generate steady demand for retail, public facilities, and services in and around Baychester. Public institutions such as fire services on Eastchester Road, multiple public schools, and New York Public Library branches at East Gun Hill Road and in Co-op City further anchor daily foot traffic and stabilize local commercial patterns.

Transit infrastructure significantly supports Baychester’s commercial connectivity. The 5 train serves the neighborhood at Baychester Avenue and Gun Hill Road stations, providing direct access for workers, shoppers, and students moving between the neighborhood and other parts of the Bronx and Manhattan. A dense network of MTA bus routes—Bx25, Bx26, Bx28, Bx30, Bx31, Bx38, and the express BxM10—runs along corridors such as Allerton Avenue, Gun Hill Road, Boston Road, and Eastchester Road, tying Baychester to Co-op City, Fordham Center, Pelham Parkway, Westchester Square, Woodlawn, and Midtown Manhattan. Bee-Line routes BL60, BL61, and BL62 use Boston Road to connect the neighborhood directly with Westchester County job centers such as White Plains and Port Chester, expanding the commuter and customer catchment area for local businesses. Nearby regional medical campuses in Morris Park—Calvary Hospital, Montefiore’s Weiler Hospital, NYC Health + Hospitals/Jacobi, and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine—as well as Montefiore’s Wakefield Campus in Williamsbridge, create a broader institutional corridor accessible from Baychester by road and bus. Together, these factors make Baychester a transit-served, institutionally anchored neighborhood with steady local demand and through-traffic, suited for neighborhood retail, services, and community-oriented commercial uses along its key arterials.
