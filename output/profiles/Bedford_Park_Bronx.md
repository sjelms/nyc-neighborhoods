## Bedford Park

Bedford Park is a residential neighborhood in the northwest Bronx , New York City, adjacent to the New York Botanical Garden . Its boundaries, starting from the north and moving clockwise, are: Mosholu Parkway to the north, Webster Avenue to the east, East 196th Street to the south, and Jerome Avenue to the west.

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Bedford Park is a dense northwest Bronx neighborhood with its primary commercial spine along Bedford Park Boulevard, supported by retail and service uses along major arterials such as Jerome Avenue and Grand Concourse.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** The neighborhood’s proximity to the New York Botanical Garden, Jerome Park Reservoir, and the cluster of schools and Lehman College along the so‑called “Educational Mile” creates steady daytime institutional foot traffic that supports local commerce.


- **THE MARKET:** Land use is dominated by mid‑rise apartment buildings with a small stock of three‑story Victorian houses and very limited vacant land, keeping commercial opportunities concentrated along established corridors and transit nodes.



---

### Around the Block

Bedford Park presents as a compact, transit‑rich Bronx neighborhood whose commercial activity is tightly woven into its major roads and institutions. Bedford Park Boulevard functions as the main business district, concentrating street‑level retail and services in a walkable corridor that carries both neighborhood residents and students from nearby schools. Grand Concourse and Jerome Avenue, the two principal north–south arterials, add further retail, professional services, and local businesses, benefiting from continuous subway ridership along the 4, B, and D lines that parallel these avenues. Mosholu Parkway, partly a limited‑access roadway and partly a surface thoroughfare, frames the northern edge, channeling vehicular traffic into the area and linking local businesses to broader Bronx circulation patterns.

Institutional anchors play an outsized role in shaping Bedford Park’s commercial environment. Along the Jerome Park Reservoir sits the “Educational Mile,” a concentration that includes three major high schools—Walton, DeWitt Clinton, and Bronx Science—plus elementary schools and Lehman College. This cluster generates a large daily population of students, faculty, and staff who patronize nearby eateries, convenience stores, and service retail along Bedford Park Boulevard and adjoining streets. Lehman College itself, with its sizable, architecturally distinct campus and performance center, adds evening and weekend event traffic that can support cultural and food‑oriented businesses. Transit connectivity underpins much of this activity: multiple subway stations and extensive bus coverage, including routes that terminate in Bedford Park and Bee‑Line buses to Westchester, make the neighborhood a hub for both local and regional riders. While the built form is largely residential in height and character, the combination of arterial roadways, institutional density, and strong transit access supports a steady if localized commercial ecosystem geared toward education, health services, and day‑to‑day retail demand.
