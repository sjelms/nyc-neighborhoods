## Lenox Hill

40°46′08″N 73°57′43″W ﻿ / ﻿ 40.769°N 73.962°W ﻿ / 40.769; -73.962

---

### Key Details
- **WHAT TO EXPECT:** Lenox Hill is the southern section of the Upper East Side with significant portions in a historic district, featuring dense, high-end mixed-use building stock and major medical, cultural, and institutional anchors.


- **UNEXPECTED APPEAL:** Park Avenue and Lexington Avenue corridors support a concentration of boutiques, art galleries, five-star hotels, and museums such as the Frick Collection, creating strong foot traffic and destination appeal beyond local demand.


- **THE MARKET:** Commercial space is framed by luxury residential buildings from the 1910s and 1920s that are now very expensive, supported by high household incomes and a substantial local population, with service-oriented retail clustered east of Lexington Avenue and a strong healthcare and institutional presence.



---

### Around the Block

Lenox Hill occupies the lower section of the Upper East Side, positioned between major north–south avenues that drive much of its commercial identity. Park Avenue and Lexington Avenue both pass through the neighborhood, and these corridors host a range of boutiques, art galleries, and five-star hotels, indicating strong support for luxury retail and hospitality uses. The area also contains a significant portion of the Upper East Side Historic District, which can influence development and repositioning strategies due to landmark protections and preservation guidelines. Many residents and city maps identify the commercial heart of Lenox Hill east of Lexington Avenue, where service-oriented retail shops cluster alongside institutional and residential properties. This interplay between high-end retail, neighborhood-serving shops, and cultural venues underpins a relatively stable demand base for ground-floor and mid-block commercial spaces.

Healthcare and institutional uses are especially prominent and shape both employment patterns and ancillary commercial demand. Lenox Hill Hospital, located on East 77th Street, stands out as a major medical anchor, joined nearby by facilities such as Gracie Square Hospital, the Hospital for Special Surgery, Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital, Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, and Weill Cornell Medical Center in the broader Upper East Side context. These institutions collectively support a large daytime population of workers, patients, and visitors who patronize food and beverage, convenience retail, and personal-service establishments. Cultural destinations such as the Frick Collection add another steady stream of visitors and tourists, reinforcing the viability of gallery, restaurant, and hospitality uses. Strong transit access via multiple subway lines along Lexington Avenue and nearby Second Avenue, together with dense residential demographics and high median household income, create favorable conditions for a wide range of commercial tenants, from boutique operators to national brands seeking a high-profile Manhattan address.
